PROJECT HIGHLIGHT

Mapping Underutilized Land in Seattle

Senior Capstone Project produced in collaboration with House Our Neighbors


I completed this project during the spring quarter of my senior year at the University of Washington. My team collaborated with House Our Neighbors, a Seattle based nonprofit organization advocating for social housing which is affordable, publicly owned and mixed-income for all. Over the course of this project my team developed interactive GIS maps and data dashboards using ArcGIS and SQL to run queries in order to filter through a large, messy dataset for parcel-level information. We were tasked with identifying underutilized parcels in Seattle suitable for new social housing development, with metrics like access to various forms of transportation in mind. The project involved extensive land zoning research and data compilation, drawing from various local agencies' open data portals to build our own interactive maps, dashboards and data visualizations. The created material is being used to help House Our Neighbors advocate for greater social housing development and opportunities in Seattle.
