My name is Mike Ramsay, I graduated from the University of Washington in June 2025 with a degree in Geography: Data Science and a minor in Informatics. I am passionate about GIS and mapping technologies, transportation, mobility solutions and the application of sustainable practices to our infrastructure and supply chain networks. Currently, I am seeking a post-grad employment opportunity. I am eager to apply my skills and open to developing new skills as well. My background and experience is made up of primarily mapping and data science projects from various academic projects I completed during my undergrad. One of the larger and more recent projects I completed was in collaboration with House Our Neighbors (HON), a Seattle based nonprofit organization advocating for social housing which is affordable, publicly owned and mixed-income for all. My team and I utilized programs such as ArcGIS, QGIS and SQL to develop a series of interactive maps and data dashboards for HON. We were tasked with identifying underutilized parcels which followed a series of qualifying metrics, like access to transportation, proximity to schools and more, which deemed them suitable for new social housing development. The project involved extensive land zoning research and data analysis with SQL as we utilized various local agencies' open data portals to build our own interactive maps, dashboards and data visualizations. This experience helped improve my ability to perform data analysis and spatial reporting. It also reinforced the importance of maintaining accurate and accessible public information to support equity-based decision making surrounding complex issues, like the housing affordability crisis.


I also completed a year-long internship in July 2025 with the City of Seattle. I worked in the Solid Waste Division at Seattle Public Utilities where I was able to gain lots of hands-on experience in program implementation and data coordination. One of my main contributions to my team was the role I played in helping launch and operate the Reuse Seattle Program, supplying over 30 events around Seattle with reusable dishware during the summer of 2024. These efforts displacing tens of thousands of single use dishware and packaging from entering the waste stream. And I continued to help expand the program this past year, including my own activation project for the use of reusable cups at running races in Seattle. I got to handle the entire process of brainstorming and researching a topic to identify local running organizations who could benefit from this program. I then found a company which could supply us with reusable silicone cups and coordinated the ordering and purchasing process with them. During the first race, 1,315 reusable cups were used, significantly reducing single-use waste generated by the event. This has continued to be part of the Reuse Seattle program after my internship ended. Overall, I gained lots of professional experience as I frequently collaborate across departments and with various local businesses. This strengthened my ability to work effectively in a diverse, team-oriented environment with sustainability in mind.
