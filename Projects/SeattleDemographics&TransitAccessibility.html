Analyzing Seattle Transit Accessibility & Demographic Data

Produced during lab for GEOGRAPHY 461: Urban GIS

February 2025

Project Summary

This lab utilizes General Transit feed Specification (GTFS) schedule data from King County Metro courtesy of Sound Transit. The goal was to highlight areas underserved the greatest and compare it to demographic data to get a better picture of where in the city has the greatest need for public transit expansion. This was achieved with some buffer analysis, masking and the Census Block Groups data within ArcGIS. The data used includes three key variables that influence accessibility level needs around the city. Those being population density, households below the poverty line and households without access to a personal vehicle.



Regions with higher numbers of households below the poverty line and without access to vehicles as well as higher population densities are highlighted on both maps I created. The map on the left includes the service area within a 10 minute walk in blue and individual bus stop locations around the city. The map to the right highlights the underserved regions around the city, there are quite a few around Rainier Valley and White Center as well as around Northgate. Figure 3 above showcases the counts of each population and the share/count of that population that is not within 10 minute access to a transit stop.
