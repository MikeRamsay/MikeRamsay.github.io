Mapping King County Bus Stop Accessibility

Final Project for GEOGRAPHY 360: GIS & Mapping

November 2023 - December 2023

Project Summary

This was one of the first GIS projects I completed while utilizing QGIS. I was curious to see if there is a correlation between the availability of public transportation and the general socioeconomic status of different regions across Seattle. This is an important topic as transportation disproportionately impacts people without personal vehicles, who are usually lower income and have less transit options available. This is also a huge reason why we need high quality and affordable public transportation options available to everyone in Seattle. In order to visualize this project I created two maps, one being a choropleth map with the percentage of households below the poverty line across the different Seattle census tracts along with the locations of bus stops from King County Metro’s open data portal. The other map, to the left, was created using QGIS’s buffer analysis and clipping operations to create distance buffers around the bus stops to get a better idea of how coverage differs around Seattle. Between the two maps, there is a clear difference between North and South Seattle in terms of bus stop coverage and percentage of households below the poverty line. I believe that further analysis would be beneficial in order to confirm these beliefs but it’s clear that there is a disparity.
