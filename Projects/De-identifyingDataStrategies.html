Exploring Geomasking Mapping Methods for Sensitive Data

Project produced for GEOGRAPHY 461: Urban GIS

February 2025

Project Summary

The goal of this project was to explore various de-indentification mapping methods within ArcGIS. This is process also called geomasking which is the process of altering the location of data in an unpredictable way through the use of algorithms. These methods are primarily used when dealing with sensitive geospatial data, such as sensitive information on a patient’s overall health or treatment they are undergoing. The need to separate the identifiable information from an individual’s sensitive information is becoming increasingly important as more of our information is being digitized in an increasingly polarized world. However, one issue with these methods is at smaller scales the individual points become more visible. One way to avoid this is with hexbins (a) which does not identify individual points.
