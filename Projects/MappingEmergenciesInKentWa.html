Mapping Fire and Emergency Medical Incidents in Kent, WA

Produced during lab for GEOGRAPHY 461: Urban GIS

February 2025

Project Summary

The main focus of this lab was to map Kent Fire Department’s emergency calls by creating heatmaps for each type of emergency. These maps were produced in ArcGIS utilizing the spatial analyst kernel density geoprocessing tool and contour polyline datasets were added to help visualize the distribution of calls. As far as the spatial differences there are hotspots in every around downtown Kent and the neighboring East Hill neighborhood. This makes sense spatially since these regions, especially downtown Kent, are the most densely populated regions and contain lots of commercial and office buildings which attract lots of people during the day. Breaking the data into different classes (emergency types) can provide some valuable insight into where different emergencies are occurring and is usually heavily linked to local infastructure and city zoning. The kernel density and contour interval map combination can help visualize these emergency call data points in a more clear and concise manner by limiting overcrowding of data points and improving the map’s overall readability.
