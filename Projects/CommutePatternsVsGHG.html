Analyzing Seattle Commute Patterns and Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Produced for GEOGRAPHY 458: Advanced Digital Geographies

November 2024 - December 2024

Project Summary

The goal of this project was to visualize the relationship between commute patterns and greenhouse gas emissions in the Puget Sound Region with Mapbox. With the intent of exploring the impact that different forms of transportation have on emissions and to explore what this data tells us about the land use in Seattle. In terms of data used, I ended up using two datasets - both from The Washington Department of Transportation. One contains line data with 2019 average annual daily traffic (AADT) volumes across Washingtonâ€™s State Highway system. The other contains data from Washington State Employee Commute Trip Reduction Survey which is an initiative to reduce traffic and air pollution in the state by shifting solo-driving commutes to alternative forms of transportation. This provided the average daily GHG emissions produced per employee. The final result was a story map with the AADT value data and GHG emissions per employee choropleth map.
