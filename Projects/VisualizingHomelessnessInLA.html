Mapping Populations in LA at the Greatest Risk of Becoming Homeless

Produced during lab for GEOGRAPHY 461: Urban GIS

February 2025

Project Summary

This project explored the populations in Los Angeles at the greatest risk at becoming homeless and some of the risk factors associated with them. A series of maps and charts were created to visualize these statistics.

For the risk map, to the left, a target or “worst case” tract was created by isolating a single polygon tract and adding all of the worst case values to its attribute table. The similarity tool was used to determine how close the other tracts were to these values and with some editing of the symbology, the areas at higher risks for generating new homelessness were highlighted. I would say that this is an overall effective method for determining high risk areas, especially with population being factored in with the adjustments in transparency. This ensures that larger, more rural tracts, which may have smaller populations, are properly visualized in comparison to smaller tracts with larger populations around Downtown LA for example.

The prioritizing prevention visualization (to the right) focuses on metrics that are most likely to contribute the most to creating larger homeless populations. Graduated symbol maps are good for easily visualizing these hotspots, as seen with the mapping of the unemployment and rent burdened fields. While it can be hard to determine the exact bin or amount the symbols are categorized in when using these types of maps, they are still good at highlighting the general hotspots. The separation of the different fields allows for unique targeted intervention strategies for each region.


The above charts showcase some of the more indepth statistics that can be generated with ArcGIS. They explore the total homeless populations broken down by community, greatest risk factors and types of shelters in some selected neighborhoods. The highlighted neighborhoods can easily be altered by editing which are selected in the attribute table.
