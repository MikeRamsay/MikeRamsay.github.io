Seattle Health Equity Index Mapping

Produced during lab for GEOGRAPHY 461: Urban GIS

January 2025

Project Summary

For this project, the primary focuses were creating an index in ArcGIS and equity mapping which is the process of identifying disparities in access to resources or outcomes across a population. There are many benefits that can be drawn from this form of spatial analysis. One of the main benefits are the potential improvements to resource allocation by identifying areas that are in the most need of funds or services. To the right is the final composite index map I made with the composite index tool in ArcGIS. I weighted the Health 8 variable (percent of insured civilian noninstitutionalized population) by a factor of 2x compared to the other variables which included the percentage of; adults with a disability, adults reporting mental health as not good, adults with obesity and life expectancy at birth.


In response to this change in weight factor, most census tract regions grew darker or moved up in equity score - which is not a good thing in this case. These changes are most apparent around Northgate and in the south in the Rainier Valley region as seen in the map below. That means this variable impacts the equity index across the city the most due to large proportions of the population not being insured. However, interestingly enough, one region that actually saw a reduction in equity score was West Seattle.
